Features of TRiM

Our distinguishing components

Psycho-educational

TRiM educates personnel on psychological functioning in stressful situations and enhances their understanding on mental health issues at the workplace.

Peer Delivered

TRiM practitioners are normal staff members trained to assess risk of psychological harm in their peers. This ensures employees quick and confidential access to help when needed.

Attitude Modifying

Admitting mental health issues can be associated with perceiving shame and fear of being treated differently by other staff. TRiM has been found to enhance peersâ€™ help-seeking thanks to modifying attitudes to stress and mental health problems.

Teamwork Based

TRiM involves effective communication and cooperation among team members, which improves organisational functioning.

Rapid Intervention

The aim of TRiM is to identify potential psychological problems following a traumatic event and refer them for early intervention, which facilitates treatment and earlier return to work.
